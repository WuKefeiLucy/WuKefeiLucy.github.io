2020-12-22 Book books Justice Comments

Justice-second article

The article records some of my thoughts after reading the third article in Justice: What’s the Right Thing To Do.
Here are some new ideas about injuries and rewards to soldiers who took part in wars.

SOME EXPLAINATIONS

Purple Heart: A medal given to soldiers who are wonded or killed in a war.
PTSD: Post-traumatic stress disorder. A mental injury caused by serious traumatic events. It is related to many sectors, including gender, age, social status, characteristics, violence, etc. It is extremely unpredictable.

MY OPINIONS

First of all, we need to know that the Purple Heart Medal is awarded to soldiers who have been injured or killed in the war. This is recorded in American law.
Well, here we need to discuss whether mental illness is a kind of trauma. In fact, mental illness is caused by external stimuli (which often cause great damage to people’s cognition) and some thoughts in people’s mind.

If you are a soldier, you are sent to the battlefield in Iraq. It’s exciting to have real guns and ammunition there. However, it will not be long before you find out that the injuries caused by these guns and ammunition are also real. You cannot escape from the explosion, as well as shots, in a real battle field. One by one, your friends are knocked down by stray bullets and moan in front of you. The smoke of the explosion was mixed with the remains of the broken soldiers. The smell of scorch and blood almost made you spit out.

You see, it is very painful just to imagine the scenes through the above words, not to mention those who have experienced these scenes themselves. Obviously, this has been able to cause mental disorders and even mental illness, and one of the most common is PTSD.

However, some soldiers, especially those with multiple old wounds, will support another view.
In their eyes, suffering from mental illness is not the enemy’s harm to you, but you can’t bear the pressure you have to bear as a soldier. They consider PTSD a sign of cowardice and insist that the purple heart should not be awarded to soldiers with mental illness.
Their reason is that mental illness cannot be evaluated or measured, so whether it exists or not cannot be confirmed, so the certificate of awarding the Purple Heart Medal cannot be confirmed.This seems to make some sense. However, in fact, people with mental illness are tortured by it all their lives. For them, those horrible scenes are repeated again and again, and they may be trapped in the battlefield where they have been injured all their lives.It’s self-evident whether it’s trauma or not.

The significance of my reading this article is not to judge whether the United States should award the Purple Heart Medal to soldiers suffering from mental illness. In fact, that is not something I can criticize or change alone. I did not give a definite answer. Maybe everyone has the value of judgment, and I cannot comment on it.
