Artist interview:If you can still be like a child when you grow up

2021-02-07 study experience study experience Comments

When I called artist Gao Xinrong, I could hear a sound of chewing crunchy snacks.

Originally from Nanjing, capital of east China’s Jiangsu Province, the 25-year-old artist began to learn painting since five, but shelved the skill after going to her university. In the winter of 2018, in order to leave unhappiness behind, she started to paint again.

“My paintings are all about making people forget their unhappiness and recall their sweet memories.” she concluded her art pieces.

For the artist, sweets, snacks and happy childhood memories are the origin of her creativity.

“I like eating all the time, especially when I have no idea, sweet and snacks will help me come up with ideas,” she said.

Referring to subjects of her digital paintings, she showed me two pieces. One is a cake and another is a child. “I think my paintings are cute and childlike,” she said with a smile.

“Memories of Childhood” .Courtesy of Gao Xinrong

The first piece — titled “memories of childhood” — features a little girl who rides on a bicycle and laughs happily, featuring a little old-fashioned tone. The whole scene is based on her grandfather’s hometown with retro color and ancient charm, including many small details that carry her childhood memory such as colorful stickers and tin cans of biscuits.

“South Korean retro cakes” xxx (year). Courtesy of Gao Xinrong

Another one — which she titled “South Korean retro cakes” — features two retro cakes with pigs and colorful cream toppings. She always wanted to taste authentic South Korean retro cakes which are very popular this year, but failed to find them in Nanjing. Hence, she decided to draw them.

“Sweet things can cure me completely. When I’m unhappy and lack of inspirations, as long as I see this kind of cake, it will make me feel very happy.”

In 2018, the artist set up her own art studio Xinwen Lianbo in the city. According to her, “Xin” and “Wen” come from her and her husband’s names, and “Lianbo” means “alliance, encouragement and companionship.”

“If there is a chance in the future, I want to turn this studio into a painting salon, where you can relax, chat, draw, create handicrafts and enjoy afternoon tea,” Gao told me her expectation.

“I hope you will meet your love here,” said Gao.“I hope people who have seen my paintings can have sweet childhood and live happily like children in the future.”
